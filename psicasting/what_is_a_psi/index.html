What is a Psi?

A psi is a discrete psionic effect, a single shaping of the psionic energies that suffuse the multiverse into a specific, limited expression. In casting a psi, a character carefully plucks at the invisible strands of raw psionics suffusing the world, pins them in place in a particular pattern, sets them vibrating in a specific way, and then releases them to unleash the desired effect--in most cases, all in the span of seconds.
Psi powers can be versatile tools, weapons, or protective wards. They can deal damage or undo it, impose or remove conditions, drain life energy away, and restore life to the dead.
Uncounted thousands of psi powers have been created over the course of the multiverse's history, and many of them are long forgotten. Some might yet lie recorded in crumbling psi archives hidden in ancient ruins or trapped in the minds of dead gods. Or they might someday be reinvented by a character who has amassed enough power and wisdom to do so.

Psi Level

Every psi has a level from 0 to 9. A psi power's level is a general indicator of how powerful it is, with the lowly (but still impressive) psionic missile at 1st level and the earth-shaking wish at 9th. Cantrips--simple but powerful psi powers that characters can cast almost by rote--are level 0. The higher a psi power's level, the higher level a psicaster must be to use that psi.
Psi level and character level don't correspond directly. Typically, a character has to be at least 17th level, not 9th level, to cast a 9th-level psi.

Known and Prepared Psi Powers

Before a psicaster can use a psi, he or she must have the psi firmly fixed in mind, or must have access to the psi in a psionic item. Members of a few classes, including Psi-Stars and Psionics, have a limited list of psi powers they know that are always fixed in mind. The same thing is true of many psionics-using monsters. Other psicasters, such as Medics and Technomancers, undergo a process of preparing psi powers. This process varies for different classes, as detailed in their descriptions.
In every case, the number of psi powers a caster can have fixed in mind at any given time depends on the character's level.

Psi Slots

Regardless of how many psi powers a caster knows or prepares, he or she can cast only a limited number of psi powers before resting. Manipulating the fabric of psionics and channeling its energy into even a simple psi is physically and mentally taxing, and higher-level psi powers are even more so. Thus, each psicasting class's description (except that of the Symbiote) includes a table showing how many psi slots of each psi level a character can use at each character level. For example, the 3rd-level Technomancer Umara has four 1st-level psi slots and two 2nd-level slots.
When a character casts a psi, he or she expends a slot of that psi power's level or higher, effectively "filling" a slot with the psi. You can think of a psi slot as a groove of a certain size--small for a 1st-level slot, larger for a psi of higher level. A 1st-level psi fits into a slot of any size, but a 9th-level psi fits only in a 9th-level slot. So when Umara casts psionic missile, a 1st-level psi, she spends one of her four 1st-level slots and has three remaining.
Finishing a long rest restores any expended psi slots.
Some characters and monsters have special abilities that let them cast psi powers without using psi slots. For example, an Operative who follows the Way of the Four Elements, a Symbiote who chooses certain eldritch invocations, and a pit fiend from the Nine Hells can all cast psi powers in such a way.

Casting a Psi Power at a Higher Level

When a psicaster casts a psi using a slot that is of a higher level than the psi, the psi assumes the higher level for that casting. For instance, if Umara casts psionic missile using one of her 2nd-level slots, that psionic missile is 2nd level. Effectively, the psi expands to fill the slot it is put into.
Some psi powers, such as psionic missile and cure wounds, have more powerful effects when cast at a higher level, as detailed in a psi power's description.

Casting in Armor

Because of the mental focus and precise gestures required for psicasting, you must be proficient with the armor you are wearing to cast a psi. You are otherwise too distracted and physically hampered by your armor for psicasting.

Cantrips

A cantrip is a psi that can be cast at will, without using a psi slot and without being prepared in advance. Repeated practice has fixed the psi in the caster's mind and infused the caster with the psionics needed to produce the effect over and over. A cantrip's psi level is 0.

Rituals

Certain psi powers have a special tag: ritual. Such a psi can be cast following the normal rules for psicasting, or the psi can be cast as a ritual. The ritual version of a psi takes 10 minutes longer to cast than normal. It also doesn't expend a psi slot, which means the ritual version of a psi can't be cast at a higher level.
To cast a psi as a ritual, a psicaster must have a feature that grants the ability to do so. The Medic and the Naturalist, for example, have such a feature. The caster must also have the psi prepared or on his or her list of psi powers known, unless the character's ritual feature specifies otherwise, as the Technomancer's does.
